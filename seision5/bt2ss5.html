Taylor Swift


Taylor Alison Swift (born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter. She is known for her autobiographical songwriting, artistic reinventions, and cultural impact. Swift is a leading figure in popular music and has a massive fanbase known as Swifties.


She signed with Big Machine Records in 2005 and debuted as a country singer with the albums Taylor Swift (2006) and Fearless (2008). Her songs "Teardrops on My Guitar", "Love Story", and "You Belong with Me" gained her widespread recognition across both country and pop radio formats. Swift later experimented with different musical styles, incorporating rock in Speak Now (2010) and electronic elements in Red (2012). She fully transitioned to pop music with 1989 (2014) and explored hip-hop influences in Reputation (2017). Many of her songs have topped the Billboard Hot 100 charts, including "Shake It Off", "Blank Space", "Bad Blood", "Look What You Made Me Do", and "We Are Never Ever Getting Back Together".
